Uruguayan tourism has been strongly influenced by Europe and neighboring countries. Many buildings in Montevideo and Colonia del Sacramento have Spanish, Italian and Portuguese styles. This European architecture makes the cities attractive to visitors from around the world.

‚öΩ Sports

Football (soccer) was brought to Uruguay by English immigrants and sailors in the late 19th century. Today, it is the most popular sport in the country and part of the national identity.

Rugby and tennis were also introduced by the British community. Thanks to these international influences, sports clubs and stadiums like Estadio Centenario were created.

The mix of European, British and neighboring influences helped shape tourism and sports in Uruguay. These foreign roots are an important part of what makes the culture unique.
